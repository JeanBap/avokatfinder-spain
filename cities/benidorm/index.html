Living and Buying Property in Benidorm

Benidorm (Costa Blanca, Alicante) has an established international community representing approximately 40% of residents. Known for skyscraper skyline, mass tourism infrastructure, affordable apartments, it attracts buyers from across northern Europe. Average property prices stand at EUR 1,700/m2.

Buying in Spain requires completing several legal steps: obtaining an NIE number, checking the Land Registry Nota Simple, verifying community debts, and paying regional transfer tax. A local English-speaking lawyer in Benidorm manages the entire process from reservation to completion and beyond.

With a population of approximately 72,000, Benidorm has well-developed legal, banking, and administrative infrastructure. Local lawyers routinely handle conveyancing, inheritance planning, residency applications, and annual tax compliance for the large foreign owner community.

Frequently Asked Questions: Lawyers in Benidorm

Is Benidorm a good place to buy property as an investment?

Benidorm has one of the highest occupancy rates for tourist accommodation in Spain. The British and German tourist market is well-established. However, the tourist rental licence market is competitive and some older apartment blocks have restrictions on short-term lets. Your lawyer checks community statutes and VUT licence entitlement before you invest based on rental income projections.

What is the average yield for rental properties in Benidorm?

Gross rental yields in Benidorm have historically run at 5-8% for tourist-licence properties. However, these figures are before tax, management costs, and community fees. Your lawyer advises on the legal structure for rental income, Spanish income tax obligations for non-residents, and tourist licence conditions.

How long does a Benidorm property purchase take?

Typically 6-10 weeks from offer acceptance to completion. Your lawyer orders the Nota Simple, checks for debts, applies for your NIE, reviews the private purchase contract (arras), and manages notary completion. Off-plan purchases in Benidorm's new developments take longer and require additional stage-payment guarantee checks.

Are there community of owners rules that affect tourist rentals in Benidorm?

Yes. Under Spain's Horizontal Property Law (LPH), a community of owners can vote to prohibit or restrict tourist rental activity with a 3/5 majority. In Benidorm, some older residential blocks have passed such resolutions. Your lawyer obtains the community statutes and minutes of recent meetings to check for any such restrictions before completion.

What NIE number do I need to buy in Benidorm?

All foreign buyers require an NIE (Numero de Identificacion de Extranjero) before signing any purchase contract. Your lawyer applies for the NIE on your behalf at the Alicante Foreigners Office or using a Power of Attorney. The process typically takes 1-3 weeks. EU citizens use the same NIE process as non-EU citizens for property purchases.
