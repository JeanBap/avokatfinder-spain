Living and Buying Property in Murcia

Murcia (Region of Murcia) has a growing international community representing approximately 14% of residents. Known for most affordable major city in Spain, La Manga resort area, new international airport, it is increasingly on the radar of foreign buyers seeking value and quality of life in Spain. Average property prices stand at EUR 1,100/m2.

Buying property in Spain involves legal steps specific to the country: obtaining an NIE number, a Nota Simple check, verification of community debts, and payment of regional transfer tax. A local English-speaking lawyer in Murcia manages every step and ensures you never sign a document you do not fully understand.

With a population of approximately 460,000, Murcia has well-developed legal services and banking infrastructure. Local lawyers are experienced in international buyer transactions and handle conveyancing, inheritance planning, and residency applications as routine matters.

Frequently Asked Questions: Lawyers in Murcia

Why is Murcia one of Spain's most affordable property markets?

Murcia city itself is less international than coastal cities, keeping demand and prices low. However, the Region of Murcia also includes La Manga del Mar Menor (a popular resort strip) and Aguilas on the coast, which attract foreign buyers at higher price points. Murcia's international airport (Corvera) opened in 2019 and has improved connectivity significantly.

What is the transfer tax rate in the Region of Murcia?

Murcia applies a flat ITP rate of 8% on resale residential property, lower than Andalusia and the Balearics for higher-value properties. New builds attract VAT at 10% plus AJD stamp duty at 1.5%. Your lawyer files the ITP return with the Murcia regional tax authority within 30 days of completion.

What legal checks are specific to La Manga and Mar Menor?

La Manga is a narrow strip of land between the Mar Menor lagoon and the Mediterranean. Some properties sit in protected coastal zones (DPMT) with building restrictions. The Mar Menor is an ecologically protected area and new builds near its shores have faced planning challenges. Your lawyer checks coastal protection classifications and any environmental restrictions before purchase.

Are there English-speaking lawyers in Murcia?

Yes. The international community in the Region of Murcia is spread between Murcia city, La Manga, and coastal towns like Mazarron and Aguilas. English-speaking lawyers experienced in foreign buyer conveyancing are available, though less numerous than on the Costa del Sol. Avokatfinder lists verified lawyers with confirmed English language ability.

What residency options are available if I buy in Murcia?

All standard Spanish residency options apply: Non-Lucrative Visa (retirement/passive income), Digital Nomad Visa (remote workers), and Golden Visa for purchases over EUR 500,000. The Region of Murcia has no special regional residency provisions, but the lower property prices make it possible to achieve permanent residency through real estate investment at a lower total cost than coastal alternatives.
