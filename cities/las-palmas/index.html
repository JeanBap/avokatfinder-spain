Living and Buying Property in Las Palmas de Gran Canaria

Las Palmas de Gran Canaria (Gran Canaria, Canary Islands) has an established international community representing approximately 18% of residents. Known for year-round 22C temperature, digital nomad hub, Las Canteras urban beach, it attracts buyers from across northern Europe. Average property prices stand at EUR 1,900/m2.

Buying in Spain requires completing several legal steps: obtaining an NIE number, checking the Land Registry Nota Simple, verifying community debts, and paying regional transfer tax. A local English-speaking lawyer in Las Palmas de Gran Canaria manages the entire process from reservation to completion and beyond.

With a population of approximately 380,000, Las Palmas de Gran Canaria has well-developed legal, banking, and administrative infrastructure. Local lawyers routinely handle conveyancing, inheritance planning, residency applications, and annual tax compliance for the large foreign owner community.

Frequently Asked Questions: Lawyers in Las Palmas de Gran Canaria

What tax applies when buying property in Las Palmas?

The Canary Islands use their own tax system (IGIC) instead of mainland VAT. For resale property purchases, the applicable tax is ITPAJD (transfer tax) at a reduced Canary Islands rate, effectively 6.5% for properties up to EUR 300,000. Your lawyer calculates and files the tax return with the Canary Islands tax authority.

Is Las Palmas a good base for digital nomads?

Las Palmas has become one of Europe's top digital nomad destinations. Year-round mild climate, fibre internet, coworking spaces, and relatively low cost of living make it attractive. The Spanish Digital Nomad Visa (2023) allows qualifying remote workers to live legally in Spain including the Canary Islands. Your lawyer handles the visa application alongside any property purchase.

Are there specific legal considerations for Canary Islands property?

Yes. The Canary Islands have devolved taxation and some devolved planning rules. Rural land classification (suelo rustico) is strictly enforced. Some coastal properties have maritime zone (DPMT) restrictions. Your lawyer checks all applicable Canary Islands regulations alongside national Spanish property law.

What are the running costs of owning property in Las Palmas?

IBI (council tax) in Las Palmas typically runs EUR 200-600/year. Community fees for apartments average EUR 50-150/month. Non-resident owners pay IRNR annually on imputed income; your lawyer can file this return. The Canary Islands also charge a local IGIC on some services at 7% (lower than mainland VAT).
