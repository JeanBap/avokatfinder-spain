Living and Buying Property in Palma de Mallorca

Palma de Mallorca (Balearic Islands, Mallorca) is home to a growing international community of approximately 22% of residents. Known for Mediterranean climate, German and British communities, luxury marina, it is a consistently popular destination for property buyers from northern Europe. Average prices stand at EUR 3,800/m2, with strong demand in central and seafront locations.

All foreign property purchases in Spain require an NIE number, a Nota Simple check at the Land Registry, verification of community debts, and payment of regional transfer tax. A local English-speaking lawyer in Palma de Mallorca handles the entire process and ensures you are never asked to sign a document you do not understand.

With a population of approximately 420,000, Palma de Mallorca offers a full range of legal services, banking, and infrastructure. The local legal profession is experienced in international transactions and routinely handles conveyancing, inheritance planning, and residency applications for foreign nationals.

Frequently Asked Questions: Lawyers in Palma de Mallorca

What makes Palma de Mallorca a top destination for foreign buyers?

Palma combines an international airport with direct routes across Europe, a sophisticated city with Michelin-starred restaurants and a vibrant arts scene, and access to over 200km of coastline. The large German and British communities make English and German-speaking legal services widely available.

What is the Balearic Islands transfer tax (ITP) for Palma?

The Balearics use a progressive ITP scale: 8% up to EUR 400,000, 9% from EUR 400,000-600,000, 10% from EUR 600,000-1,000,000, 12% from EUR 1,000,000-2,000,000, and 13% above EUR 2,000,000. Your lawyer calculates and files the tax return with the Balearic Tax Agency.

Can I get a tourist rental licence in Palma?

Palma city centre properties have significant restrictions on tourist rental licences. The Balearic government operates a licence cap and has suspended new urban tourist rental licences in several zones. Properties with existing licences command a premium. Your lawyer verifies the current status before you purchase with rental income in mind.

Are there property types that are restricted in Palma?

Rural finca purchases near protected natural areas require checks on ANEI (Area Natural d'Especial Interes) classification. Old town properties may have heritage restrictions. Apartment buildings near the seafront have stricter renovation rules. Your lawyer identifies all applicable restrictions in the pre-contract due diligence phase.

How long does conveyancing take in Palma?

Typically 6-10 weeks for standard apartment purchases, and 10-16 weeks for complex luxury villas or properties requiring tourist licence verification. The Balearic Land Registry can be slower than mainland Spain during peak season. Your lawyer monitors progress and manages timelines.
