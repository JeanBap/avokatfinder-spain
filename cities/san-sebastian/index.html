Living and Buying Property in San Sebastian

San Sebastian (Basque Country) is one of Spain's most internationally diverse communities, with approximately 10% of residents from outside Spain. It is known for world-class gastronomy, La Concha beach, prestigious Basque lifestyle. Average property prices stand at EUR 4,200/m2, with consistent demand across all market segments.

Buying in Spain requires an NIE number, a Land Registry check (Nota Simple), verification of community debts, and payment of transfer tax. A local English-speaking lawyer manages every step, protecting you from incomplete disclosures and ensuring full legal compliance at completion.

With a population of approximately 185,000 and well-established legal and banking services, San Sebastian has everything international buyers need. Local lawyers regularly handle conveyancing, Spanish will preparation, residency applications, and ongoing tax compliance for foreign property owners.

Frequently Asked Questions: Lawyers in San Sebastian

Does the Basque Country have different tax rules for property buyers?

Yes. The Basque Country (Gipuzkoa, Bizkaia, and Alava provinces) has fiscal autonomy from the Spanish central government under the Concierto Economico. The transfer tax (ITP) rate is 4% in San Sebastian, significantly lower than Andalusia or the Balearics. The Basque tax authority (Hacienda Foral) collects and manages these taxes.

Why are property prices so high in San Sebastian?

San Sebastian consistently ranks as the most expensive city per square metre in Spain outside Madrid and Barcelona. Demand from wealthy Basques and Spanish buyers, a constrained supply of buildable land between the sea and mountains, and the city's international reputation for gastronomy and quality of life all sustain premium prices.

What is the process for non-Spanish residents buying in San Sebastian?

The process mirrors the rest of Spain: obtain an NIE, sign a reservation and private purchase contract (arras), conduct due diligence, and complete before a notary. However, Basque foral regulations apply to inheritance and community rules. Your lawyer must be versed in Basque regional law, not just general Spanish law.

What languages do lawyers in San Sebastian speak?

Spanish and Basque (Euskara) are the official languages of the Basque Country. Many San Sebastian lawyers also speak French given the city's proximity to France. English-speaking lawyers exist but are fewer than in coastal tourist hotspots. Avokatfinder shows verified language competencies so you can filter appropriately.

Is San Sebastian suitable for rental investment?

San Sebastian has a strong short-term rental market given its international tourism profile and the Semana Grande and Jazzaldia festivals. The Basque Government regulates tourist rentals and requires VFT registration. Long-term rental demand from university students and professionals is also solid year-round.
