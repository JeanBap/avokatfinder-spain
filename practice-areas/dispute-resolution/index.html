Spanish Dispute Resolution: Courts, ADR & Cost Guide (2025)

Spanish civil litigation is governed by the Ley de Enjuiciamiento Civil (LEC 1/2000). The main procedures are the juicio verbal (claims up to €15,000) and juicio ordinario (claims above €15,000). The proceso monitorio (LEC art. 812) allows debt recovery of any amount without a full trial when the debt is documented.

Tasas judiciales (LO 10/2012): Individuals are exempt from court fees since 2015 (RDL 1/2015). Legal persons (companies) still pay. Condena en costas: Spanish civil courts apply the "loser pays" rule (LEC art. 394) for the first instance — if you win on all claims, the other party pays your abogado and procurador fees.

🔍 TL;DR — Spanish Dispute Resolution
  • Proceso monitorio: fastest debt recovery — file with documents, automatic order if no 20-day opposition
  • Individuals exempt from court fees (tasas judiciales) since 2015
  • Loser-pays (costas) applies at first instance — if you win on all claims, other party pays your abogado
  • Mediación settlement homologated by court = directly enforceable without further proceedings
  • CAM arbitration: preferred for Madrid-seated international commercial disputes
