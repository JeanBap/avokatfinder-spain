Licencia de Primera Ocupación (First Occupancy Licence)

The Spanish municipal certificate confirming that a newly built or extensively reformed property complies with planning conditions and is legally habitable.

Home › Legal Glossary › Licencia de Primera Ocupación (First Occupancy Licence)
TL;DR The Spanish municipal certificate confirming that a newly built or extensively reformed property complies with planning conditions and is legally habitable.

The licencia de primera ocupación (first occupancy licence — also called cédula de habitabilidad or licencia de ocupación in some regions) is a municipal document issued by the ayuntamiento (town hall) confirming that a newly built or substantially reformed property has been built in accordance with its approved plans and complies with the habitability and safety standards required by planning law.

Without a valid licencia de primera ocupación, a property cannot be legally connected to water, electricity, and sewage utilities. Banks will typically refuse to grant a mortgage on a property without one, and notaries may decline to authenticate the purchase deed. Buying a property without this licence is a significant legal risk.

The application for the licence is made by the developer (promotor) or owner after construction is complete, submitting the Certificado Final de Obra (end-of-works certificate) signed by the project architect and certifying that the building matches the approved plans. The ayuntamiento then inspects and issues the licence, typically within one to three months.

In some regions (particularly Andalucía, Valencia, and the Canary Islands), there is a well-documented history of properties being sold without valid licences — particularly in rural areas where planning irregularities were common in the 1990s and 2000s. Buyers should verify the licence status carefully and obtain written legal advice if there is any doubt.

For existing properties being resold, the relevant document is the cédula de habitabilidad (habitability certificate), which must be renewed every few years in many regions. Check regional requirements — Catalonia, Valencia, and the Balearics have mandatory renewal requirements. An expired or absent cédula can complicate rental and utility contracts.

⚠ Common Mistake: Signing a purchase contract for a new build without confirming the licencia de primera ocupación has been applied for. Some developers transfer buyers before the licence is issued, creating delays and sometimes impossible situations if the property does not meet planning conditions.
💡 Expert Tip: For off-plan purchases, include a contractual condition allowing you to withdraw and recover your deposit if the licencia is not granted within a specified period. This is a standard protection that reputable Spanish developers will accept.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if I buy without a first occupancy licence?

Without the licence, you cannot obtain utility contracts in your name, may be unable to mortgage the property, and face potential municipal enforcement action if the property violates planning rules. In extreme cases, planning irregularities can lead to demolition orders, though this is rare for completed buildings.

Is the cédula de habitabilidad required for all property sales?

Requirements vary by autonomous community. In Catalonia, Valencia, and the Balearics, the cédula is mandatory for rental and sale. In Madrid, the equivalent document (licencia de primera ocupación) is required for new builds. Check the specific requirements for the region where the property is located.

Can the licencia be obtained after I buy?

If the property has a genuine planning irregularity, the licencia may be impossible to obtain. For properties that simply have not yet completed the administrative process, it may be obtainable with time and cost. Always clarify the exact status — "applied for" and "issued" are very different.
