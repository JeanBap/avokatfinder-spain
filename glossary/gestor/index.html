Gestor (Administrative Manager)

A Spanish gestor or gestoría — a licensed professional who handles tax filings, residency applications, vehicle registration, and administrative procedures on behalf of individuals and businesses.

Home › Legal Glossary › Gestor (Administrative Manager)
TL;DR A Spanish gestor or gestoría — a licensed professional who handles tax filings, residency applications, vehicle registration, and administrative procedures on behalf of individuals and businesses.

The gestor (gestora or gestoría) is a uniquely Spanish professional who specialises in navigating Spanish bureaucracy on behalf of individuals and companies. Unlike a lawyer (abogado), a gestor does not provide legal advice or represent clients in court — their role is administrative: filing taxes, registering vehicles, processing residency applications, handling company formation formalities, and completing the endless forms that Spanish administrative life requires.

For expats in Spain, the gestor is often the most important professional relationship after the property lawyer. A good gestor helps newly arrived foreigners obtain their NIE, register on the Padrón municipal, file annual income tax returns (IRPF), submit the Modelo 720 overseas assets declaration, and handle matters with the Hacienda (tax authority) and Seguridad Social (social security). Many gestorías offer English-language services in expat-heavy areas.

Gestors are accredited through official colegios (professional associations) — the Colegio Oficial de Gestores Administrativos. They hold professional liability insurance and are bound by professional rules. Always verify a gestor's colegiado number. Informal "facilitators" in tourist areas who claim to offer gestoría services without accreditation offer no professional protection.

Standard gestor fees for common expat tasks: NIE application assistance (€50–100), Padrón registration (€30–60), annual IRPF tax return (€80–200 depending on complexity), Modelo 720 (€150–300), self-employed (autónomo) registration (€100–200). Annual retainer packages covering routine filings typically cost €300–600/year for a straightforward expat.

For property purchases, the gestor often handles post-completion administrative tasks: payment of ITP transfer tax, submission to the Land Registry, registration of the property with the Catastro, setting up utility contracts, and annual IBI payment. Some notaries offer an integrated service through their own gestoría — check whether this is included in the notary fee or separately invoiced.

⚠ Common Mistake: Using an unaccredited "facilitator" rather than a licensed gestor. The savings are minimal and the risks are significant — incomplete filings, missed deadlines, and personal liability for errors are all real consequences.
💡 Expert Tip: Ask your gestor specifically whether they handle Modelo 720 declarations — the overseas assets declaration for Spanish residents with more than €50,000 abroad. Penalties for non-filing are severe and many general gestorías outsource this to specialists.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a gestor and a lawyer?

Yes, for different things. The lawyer handles legal due diligence, contract review, and dispute resolution. The gestor handles ongoing administrative and tax filings. In practice, many expats find a good gestoría that also employs an abogado and can handle both — but confirm who is doing what and in what capacity.

Can a gestor represent me at the tax office (Hacienda)?

Yes. Gestores are authorised to represent clients in administrative proceedings before the Agencia Tributaria (Hacienda) and other public bodies. They can respond to tax inquiries, submit appeals (recursos), and negotiate payment plans on your behalf.

What is a gestoría de coches?

A gestoría de coches specialises specifically in vehicle registration, transfer of ownership (cambio de titularidad), and ITV (MOT equivalent) administration. If you are importing a car to Spain or buying a second-hand vehicle, this specialist type of gestoría handles the Tráfico procedures that general gestorías often do not.
