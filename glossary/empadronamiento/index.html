What is Empadronamiento (Certificado de Empadronamiento)?

Spain's municipal registration — enrolling on the local council's register of inhabitants, required for accessing many public services and benefits.

Key Facts — Empadronamiento (Certificado de Empadronamiento)
  • Municipal registration — separate from residency visa or TIE
  • Free of charge at your local ayuntamiento
  • Required for: TIE application, school enrolment, GP registration, many benefits
  • Certificate valid for 3 months for official purposes
  • Update when you move address; request baja on leaving Spain

Empadronamiento is the process of registering your residence with the local Spanish municipality (ayuntamiento). Once registered, you receive a certificado de empadronamiento or certificado de residencia — an official document confirming your address. The empadronamiento is separate from immigration and residency law; it is a purely administrative municipal registration.

In practice, the certificado de empadronamiento is required for a surprisingly wide range of administrative processes in Spain. You typically need it to apply for a TIE residency card, enrol children in state schools, register with a local GP (médico de cabecera), apply for certain benefits, vote in local elections (for EU citizens), obtain driving licence equivalence, and in many regions for connecting utilities in your name.

You empadronar yourself at the local padron (census office) of your ayuntamiento. You will need: your passport or TIE, documentation proving your address (a rental contract, utility bill in your name, or — for property owners — the escritura of your property). The process is free and straightforward. You can register even if you are not yet a legal resident — the empadronamiento is not a residency permit.

The certificado de empadronamiento typically has a validity period of three months for official use. You may need to obtain a new certificate each time it is required for a formal process, even if your address has not changed. Most ayuntamientos provide the certificate within minutes of application.

If you move address within Spain, you must update your empadronamiento at the new municipality. If you leave Spain permanently, you should request a baja (deregistration). Maintaining an empadronamiento at an address where you no longer live is technically incorrect, though rarely enforced.

Common Mistake: Confusing empadronamiento with residency. Being empadronado does not give you the right to live in Spain — it only registers where you live. Equally, you can and should register even before you obtain formal residency status, as it is required for the TIE application itself.
Expert Tip: Some ayuntamientos require an appointment (cita previa) for empadronamiento while others accept walk-ins. Check your local council's website or call ahead before visiting. In tourist-heavy areas, appointments can take 2–3 weeks to obtain.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can non-EU citizens register on the empadronamiento?

Yes. Any person living in Spain, regardless of nationality, immigration status, or whether they own or rent their home, has both the right and the legal obligation to register on the padrón municipal. The empadronamiento is specifically not a residency or immigration document.

Does my empadronamiento need to match my TIE address?

Your TIE should reflect your current registered address. When you update your empadronamiento after a move, you should also notify the Extranjería of your address change, as your TIE records an address and you are legally required to notify changes.

What if my landlord refuses to let me use their address for empadronamiento?

Landlords cannot legally prevent tenants from registering. If your landlord refuses to provide the necessary documentation, contact your local ayuntamiento — some municipalities have procedures to register tenants who face this problem, including requesting a housing inspection.
