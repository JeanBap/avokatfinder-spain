Modelo 720 (Overseas Assets Declaration)

Spain's mandatory annual declaration of overseas assets held by Spanish tax residents — applicable to assets exceeding €50,000 per category.

Home › Legal Glossary › Modelo 720 (Overseas Assets Declaration)
TL;DR Spain's mandatory annual declaration of overseas assets held by Spanish tax residents — applicable to assets exceeding €50,000 per category.

The Modelo 720 is Spain's annual overseas assets declaration, legally required for all Spanish tax residents who hold assets abroad exceeding €50,000 in any of three categories: bank accounts and savings, securities and investments, and real estate. It must be filed with the Agencia Tributaria (Spanish Tax Authority) between 1 January and 31 March each year for the preceding tax year.

The three asset categories are assessed independently. A Spanish resident who has €40,000 in a UK bank account, €55,000 in French investment funds, and a jointly-owned Portuguese property worth €120,000 (their share: €60,000) must declare the investment funds and the property — but not the bank account (below the €50,000 threshold). Once the threshold is crossed in any category, all assets in that category must be declared, not just those above €50,000.

The declaration must include the type and description of each asset, the country of location, the financial institution or registry details, the acquisition date, the balance or value as at 31 December, and the taxpayer's percentage ownership. Subsequent declarations are only required when a previously declared asset increases by more than €20,000, or when it is sold or transferred.

Penalties for non-filing or late filing of the Modelo 720 were notoriously severe under the original 2012 legislation — fixed penalties of €5,000 per item missed, with no cap. The European Court of Justice ruled in 2022 that the original penalties were disproportionate under EU law. Spain has subsequently reformed the penalty regime to align with standard administrative penalty proportionality rules, though compliance remains mandatory.

Filing obligations apply regardless of whether the assets were acquired before or after becoming Spanish tax resident. New arrivals to Spain should audit all their overseas holdings immediately and consult a qualified gestor or tax adviser before their first Spanish tax filing season. Many tax advisers offer a combined Modelo 720 and IRPF service for expats.

⚠ Common Mistake: Assuming the Modelo 720 only applies to large fortunes. The €50,000 threshold catches many ordinary expats — a modest investment portfolio, a foreign pension fund, and a share of the family home abroad can all trigger the obligation.
💡 Expert Tip: File even if you are uncertain whether your assets exactly cross the threshold. The cost of a precautionary filing (one gestor fee) is far lower than the risk of a penalty investigation if the tax authority discovers undeclared overseas assets through international data exchange.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Modelo 720 apply to UK assets post-Brexit?

Yes. The Modelo 720 applies to assets in any country outside Spain, including the UK after Brexit. There is no EU vs non-EU distinction for this declaration. UK bank accounts, ISAs, pensions, and UK property held by Spanish tax residents are all potentially declarable.

If I declare on Modelo 720, does Spain tax those overseas assets?

Declaring on Modelo 720 does not itself trigger Spanish taxation. The declaration is informational. Spanish tax treatment of the income or gains from overseas assets is determined by Spain's Income Tax Act (IRPF/IRNR) and relevant double taxation treaties. The 720 and your tax return are separate obligations.

What counts as a "bank account" for Modelo 720 purposes?

All accounts held at financial institutions abroad: current accounts, savings accounts, fixed-term deposits, and money market accounts. Cryptocurrency held on overseas exchanges is a grey area under current Spanish guidance — check with a tax adviser for the most current position.
